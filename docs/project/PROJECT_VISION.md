# AoTune Project Vision

## Long-Term Vision

AoTune is a personal-first AI agent workspace for Japanese music, cosplay
planning, ACGN-inspired creation, and identity-driven artifacts. It gives users
purpose-built spaces where agents can assist research, learning, planning, and
creative work without replacing the user's judgment or voice.

The product is workspace-based and artifact-centered. Conversations may support
the process, but the durable value comes from outputs users can revisit and
refine: learning notes, research cards, cosplay plans, creative prompts, visual
concepts, and personal branding materials.

## Why AoTune?

AoTune is inspired by the quiet, blue-toned atmosphere I often feel from
Yorushika and Japanese music: calm, literary, youthful, and slightly
melancholic.

The name represents the mood the project should carry. It is not only about
songs, lyrics, or music learning. It also reflects the romantic visual language
of ACGN culture: characters, memory, summer, identity, self-expression, and the
feeling of turning personal interests into something structured and creative.

Yorushika is a personal inspiration, not an affiliation or endorsement. The
name and vision describe the final multi-workspace product, not only the first
Japanese Lyrics Learning implementation.

## Product Character

AoTune should feel calm, focused, literary, and practical. It should help users
develop a private body of work rather than compete for attention. Japanese music
and ACGN culture inform its atmosphere, while cosplay planning, creative
identity, and structured personal research broaden its purpose.

English and Chinese interfaces are planned. Future identity-aware access control
should protect workspaces and artifacts through explicit permissions and audit
logging, with possible integration into a separate IAM project when the product
requires it.
